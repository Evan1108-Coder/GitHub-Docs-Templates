# Choosing A License

A license tells other people what they are allowed to do with your project.

This guide is educational and not legal advice.

## Common Choices

- MIT: short, permissive, common for open-source projects.
- Apache-2.0: permissive and includes patent language.
- GPL-3.0: requires modified versions to stay open under the same license.
- LGPL-3.0: often used for libraries where some linking use is allowed.
- AGPL-3.0: similar to GPL, with network-service sharing requirements.
- BSD-2-Clause or BSD-3-Clause: permissive licenses with simple conditions.
- MPL-2.0: file-level copyleft license.
- Unlicense or CC0-1.0: public-domain-style options.

## Beginner Advice

If you are unsure, read the license text carefully and compare your goals:

- Do you want people to use your code with few restrictions?
- Do you want improvements to stay open source?
- Are you working with a company, school, or organization that has rules?

When the choice matters, ask someone qualified for legal advice.
